ISO 31000 provides a comprehensive framework for managing risk effectively. It outlines principles, a framework, and a structured process to help organizations identify, assess, and mitigate risks while achieving their objectives. The standard is applicable to any organization, regardless of size, sector, or industry.

Clause 4: Principles of Risk Management
Defines the core principles that underpin effective risk management, including integration, structured and comprehensive approach, customization, inclusivity, and continuous improvement.

Clause 5: Framework for Managing Risk
Describes how organizations should design, implement, and maintain a robust risk management framework. This clause emphasizes leadership and commitment, the integration of risk management into governance and decision-making, and the iterative process of monitoring and improving the framework.

Clause 6: Risk Management Process
Provides a step-by-step guide for managing risks, detailing specific activities to be performed. This clause emphasizes communication and consultation, context establishment, and the core components of risk assessment: risk identification, risk analysis, and risk evaluation.

Risk assessment, a key component of ISO 31000 Clause 6, is detailed in three sub-clauses. This process is critical for understanding and addressing risks effectively:

  • Clause 6.4.2: Risk Identification
    Involves identifying the sources of risk, potential events, and their causes and consequences.
  • Clause 6.4.3: Risk Analysis
    Focuses on understanding the nature of identified risks and their characteristics. This includes analyzing uncertainties, potential consequences, likelihood, and existing controls to estimate the level of risk.
  • Clause 6.4.4: Risk Evaluation
    Compares the results of risk analysis with established criteria to determine whether further action is required. It helps prioritize risks and decide whether to accept, treat, or monitor them.
